Disaster Recovery Planning

Helping small organizations prepare for and recover from unexpected events

Overview

I specialize in developing disaster recovery plans (DRP) tailored for small organizations. My approach ensures that critical business operations can be restored quickly and efficiently after disruptive incidents, such as cyberattacks, hardware failures, or natural disasters.

I work closely with business owners and staff to create practical, easy-to-follow plans that address their unique needs, technical capabilities, and risk profiles.

Disaster Recovery Planning Experience

  • Developed and implemented DRPs for small businesses and non-profits
  • Facilitated business impact analysis to identify critical functions and dependencies
  • Designed backup and restoration strategies for on-premises and cloud environments
  • Created step-by-step recovery procedures for various disaster scenarios
  • Trained staff on DRP execution and periodic testing
  • Assisted organizations in meeting compliance requirements related to business continuity

Generalized Disaster Recovery Planning Process

  • Preparation: Define objectives, scope, and assign roles and responsibilities
  • Asset Inventory: Identify and document all critical assets (hardware, software, data, personnel, vendors)
  • Risk Review: Assess threats and vulnerabilities specific to the organization’s environment (e.g., ransomware, power loss, fire, human error)
  • Strategy Development: Establish backup, restoration, and communication procedures
  • Plan Documentation: Create clear, actionable recovery steps and escalation paths
  • Testing & Training: Conduct regular drills and update the plan based on lessons learned

The Importance of Asset Inventory & Risk Review

A comprehensive asset inventory is the foundation of any effective disaster recovery plan. By cataloging all critical systems, data, and resources, organizations can prioritize recovery efforts and ensure nothing essential is overlooked.

Reviewing risks tailored to your environment—such as local weather threats, reliance on specific vendors, or unique business processes—enables the creation of realistic and relevant recovery strategies. This targeted approach helps minimize downtime and financial loss when incidents occur.
