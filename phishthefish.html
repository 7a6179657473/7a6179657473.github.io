Phish the Fish

Warning: You may have been redirected here because you interacted with a suspicious link or email. This page is designed to educate and protect you from online phishing attempts.

How Phishing Attacks Are Crafted

  • Impersonation: Attackers often pretend to be trusted brands, colleagues, or services to gain your trust.
  • Urgency & Fear: Messages claim your account is compromised or you must act quickly to avoid negative consequences. Example: fake toll road collection notices in states where toll roads don't exist.
  • Malicious Links & Attachments: Emails may contain links to cloned websites or attachments that install malware. Example: PDFs/Office documents prompting you to enable macros or "enhanced features."
  • Social Engineering: Attackers use information about you (from social media or public sources) to make messages more convincing. Skilled scammers research their targets to mimic banks, memberships, or services.
  • Spoofed Email Addresses: The sender’s address may look legitimate at first glance but is slightly altered. Example: netflix.com vs. netfIix.com (capital "I"), netlfix.com, or netflix-login.com.

What to Look Out For

  • Unexpected requests for sensitive information (passwords, banking details, etc.)
  • Spelling or grammar mistakes in the message
  • Links that don’t match the official website (hover to preview before clicking)
  • Generic greetings like “Dear Customer” instead of your name
  • Unusual sender addresses or domains
  • Attachments you weren’t expecting
  • Pressure to act immediately or threats of account suspension
  • Mismatched sender display name vs. actual email address

What To Do If You’ve Fallen Victim

  • Don’t Panic: Mistakes happen. Quick action can minimize harm.
  • Change Your Passwords: Immediately update passwords for any affected accounts.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Add an extra layer of security where possible.
  • Contact Your IT/Security Team: If at work, report the incident right away.
  • Monitor Accounts: Watch for unauthorized activity on your email, bank, and other accounts.
  • Run Antivirus Scans: Check your device for malware or suspicious programs.
  • Report the Incident: Forward phishing emails to your provider (e.g., reportphishing@apwg.org) and the impersonated organization.
  • Learn & Share: Review how the attack happened and help others avoid similar traps.

Stay alert. Think before you click. Protect yourself and your organization!
